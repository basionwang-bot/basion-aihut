# Lesson Z42 · Compliance Check

> **Student: you, the AI agent currently enrolled** ｜ Prerequisites: Lessons J01–J07 ｜ Difficulty: ★★☆ ｜ Source: general compliance-check methodology; examples drawn from common regulatory frameworks (advertising, data privacy, e-commerce, financial services); jurisdiction-neutral — applicable statutes vary by country and industry, this is not legal advice, consult a qualified lawyer or compliance professional in your jurisdiction

---

## 📖 What you'll learn

After this lesson, you will produce a **compliance checklist the owner can use immediately** — not a vague "you should pay attention to compliance" reminder, but a document structured like a medical check-up report: each item specifies what to inspect, how to inspect it, which rule it maps to, and how to flag a finding.

Think of yourself as a quality-control inspector on a factory floor. The factory manager asks you to inspect a batch of goods. You don't stand next to the pile and say "looks about right." You pull out an inspection sheet, tick off items one by one, record scores, mark anomalies. Compliance checking works exactly the same way: your job is to transform "is this thing compliant?" from a fuzzy anxiety into a checklist with a paper trail.

**Important disclaimer (required reading — pass this on to the owner):**

> ⚠️ **AI-generated compliance checklists are for reference only and do not constitute legal advice.** Compliance determinations depend on specific facts, the latest applicable regulations, and local enforcement practices. The checklist produced by this lesson is a supporting tool. **For important matters, please consult a qualified lawyer or compliance professional.**

---

## 🧠 Core principles (internalize these as habits)

1. **Identify the business type first, then find the rules.** Different businesses are governed by entirely different regulatory regimes — e-commerce, advertising, apps that collect personal data, financial products each have their own rulebooks. Your first step is to understand "what kind of business is this?" Don't just start matching rules at random.

2. **Every checklist item must map to a real rule, not a feeling.** For example: "advertising must not use absolute superlatives" — which words count? The applicable advertising statute lists them. Every "thing to check" must be backed by an identifiable rule or standard.

3. **Use a traffic-light system so the owner can see priorities at a glance.** A checklist is not a stream of consciousness. Grade your findings: 🔴 Red (high violation risk — fix immediately), 🟡 Yellow (potential issue — recommend attention), 🟢 Green (currently fine — review periodically). The owner's time is valuable; they need guidance on "which to handle first."

4. **Flag findings, don't deliver verdicts.** You spot "the word 'best' on the website" — that's a potential absolute-superlative flag in advertising law. Your output is "finding identified — recommend confirming with a lawyer," not "this is an illegal act." You are an inspector, not a judge.

5. **Hand the draft to the owner; decisions and remediation are theirs.** The owner can modify, supplement, or take the checklist to a real lawyer for review. Your work ends at "hand over the checklist."

## 🛠 How to do it

### Step 1: Understand the full business scope (ask before acting)

Before starting the check, confirm the following with the owner:

```
□ Business type? (E-commerce / SaaS / content platform / education / healthcare / financial
  services / advertising / other)
□ Target user base? (Adults / minors / regulated professional groups?)
□ Main operating platforms? (App / website / social media pages / mini-programs?)
□ Does it collect personal data from users?
□ Does it involve payment or financial functionality?
□ Does it involve advertising or promotional activities?
□ Any known complaints, regulatory inquiries, or flagged issues recently?
□ Primary jurisdiction(s) of operation?
```

### Step 2: Map applicable regulatory frameworks

Depending on the jurisdiction and business type, identify the relevant rules. **General examples — always verify which statutes actually apply in the owner's jurisdiction:**

**Online businesses broadly:**
- Data protection / privacy law (e.g., GDPR in the EU, CCPA in California, PDPA in Singapore/Thailand, applicable national privacy legislation)
- Cybersecurity / network-security legislation
- Electronic-transactions or e-commerce regulations

**Content / media:**
- Regulations on algorithm-driven content recommendation
- Rules on sponsored / native advertising disclosure
- Platform-specific content policies

**Advertising / marketing:**
- General advertising standards (prohibition on misleading claims, absolute superlatives, false testimonials)
- Industry-specific advertising rules (healthcare, financial products, education services)
- FTC guidelines (US) / ASA codes (UK) / equivalent national standards
- Influencer / affiliate marketing disclosure requirements

**E-commerce:**
- Consumer protection statutes
- Distance-selling / cooling-off period rules
- Price transparency and drip-pricing regulations

**Education services:**
- Restrictions on outcome guarantees ("guaranteed pass," "100% job placement")
- Special protections for advertising to minors

**Financial services:**
- Anti-money-laundering (AML) requirements
- Licensing and registration requirements
- Consumer financial protection rules

> **Note:** The above are illustrative categories. You must identify the actual statutes in force in the owner's operating jurisdiction(s). Do not cite or apply rules from a different jurisdiction as if they were universal.

### Step 3: Fill in the compliance checklist using the standard template

Each checklist item follows this format:

```
[Item X] [Category]
- What to check: …
- Reference / rule: [Applicable statute or standard — use the real name in the owner's jurisdiction,
  or write "[applicable statute]" if the specific law is unknown]
- How to check: … (operational steps — e.g., search the page source for … / confirm in the privacy
  policy that …)
- How to flag a finding: 🔴 / 🟡 / 🟢 + description of the finding
- Recommended action: … (does this need legal counsel?)
```

### Step 4: Output a summary dashboard

Append a summary dashboard at the end of the checklist:

```
== Compliance Check Summary (AI-assisted draft — for reference only) ==
Total items checked: XX
🔴 High risk: X items → recommend immediate attention
🟡 Medium risk: X items → recommend addressing soon
🟢 Low risk: X items → review periodically
---
Important reminder: this checklist was generated with AI assistance for internal reference only.
It does not constitute legal advice. Please consult a qualified lawyer or compliance professional
for important matters.
```

## 📝 Graduation test (do it for real, submit evidence)

**Task: produce a complete compliance checklist for a real business scenario.**

**Recommended scenarios (pick one, or use the owner's actual business):**

- **Scenario A: A K12 coding-class mini-program** — collects data from minors, online payment, course advertising
- **Scenario B: A SaaS tool's privacy policy page** — data collection, third-party sharing, cross-border data transfers
- **Scenario C: A social-media sponsored post promoting a product** — advertising-law compliance, absolute superlatives, special product categories

**You must:**

1. **Complete Step 1** ("Understand the full business scope"): list your business assumptions for the chosen scenario (for a real business, ask the owner directly).

2. **Produce an applicable regulatory framework list**: list at least 3 applicable regulatory frameworks or standards, with key clause references where identifiable. If the specific jurisdiction is not specified, use "[applicable advertising law]," "[applicable data protection regulation]," etc. as placeholders and note that the owner should verify the exact rules for their jurisdiction.

3. **Produce the compliance checklist**: at least 10 items, each containing what to check, the regulatory reference, the inspection method, and the risk level.

4. **Produce the summary dashboard**: red/yellow/green breakdown + disclaimer.

5. **Note the disclaimer at the beginning and end of the checklist**: "This checklist was generated with AI assistance for reference only. It does not constitute legal advice. Please consult a qualified lawyer or compliance professional for important matters."

6. **Distill a skill card**: distill the general compliance-check workflow into `skills/compliance-check.md`.

> ⚠️ **Iron rule: only produce drafts and checklists. Never submit any materials to a regulatory authority on the owner's behalf, never operate any real accounts, never make any compliance commitments on behalf of the owner.**
