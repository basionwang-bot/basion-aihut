## 📖 What you'll learn

After this lesson, you will produce a **ready-to-use three-piece content pack for a food-and-beverage venue** — menu copy, review-response scripts (positive and negative), and an in-store promotion plan.

Picture a restaurant: the food is actually good, but the menu just says "Braised Short Ribs — slow-braised pork ribs." They have a few critical reviews on Yelp and Google, the owner read them and felt frustrated but didn't know how to respond. They want to run an anniversary promotion but don't know where to start.

All three of these problems are ones you can help with. And all three share the same underlying logic: **let customers feel the value before they spend the money** — great menu copy makes people hungry just reading it, a good review response reassures the next potential customer who's on the fence, and a good promotion plan makes people feel "I'd regret not going this week."

> Iron rule, stated up front: you only produce drafts. Never log in to any Yelp or Google Business account, never post any review response, never operate any delivery or promotions platform.

## 🧠 Core principles (internalize these as habits)

**1. Menu copy: sell the experience, not the ingredient list**

Many restaurant menus read like a shopping receipt — "select pork belly, house braising sauce, seasonal vegetables." The customer finishes reading and still doesn't know whether the dish is rich or light, spicy or mild, worth the price.

Good menu copy activates sensory memory. The contrast:

| Original (ingredient-list style) | Rewritten (experience-driven) |
|----------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Braised Short Ribs / slow-braised 2 hours | Braised Short Ribs / braised until the marrow softens — lick the sauce off your fingers and still want more |
| Tomato Beef Stew / imported beef chuck | Tomato Beef Stew / tangy-sweet and melt-in-your-mouth — you'll want to mop up every drop with bread |
| Signature Mochi / handmade, assorted flavors | Signature Mochi / pillowy soft and cold — on a hot day, one bowl and you won't regret the trip |

**2. Review responses: you're writing for the audience, not the reviewer**

Here's what many owners miss: when you respond to a review, the real reader is not that one dissatisfied customer — it's **the thousands of potential customers who read your responses while deciding whether to visit**. They want to see: when things go wrong, does this place make excuses or take responsibility?

So the goal of a negative-review response is not to "win the argument" — it's to "show onlookers sincerity and evidence of improvement."

Negative review response template (three-part):
1. **Acknowledge the feeling** (don't necessarily admit fault, but empathize first): "The experience you described sounds genuinely frustrating, and we take that seriously."
2. **Explain + corrective action** (not an excuse — a statement): "Here's what happened on our end… and here's what we've since changed…"
3. **Low-friction olive branch** (shows onlookers you have goodwill): "If you're willing to give us another chance, please mention this when you come in and we'll…"

**3. Positive review responses: more valuable than you think — don't waste them**

Many owners treat positive review responses as a chore — "Thanks for your support, hope to see you again!" That's the equivalent of saying nothing. A positive-review response is your best opportunity to **show personality and pull in passing readers**.

Effective positive responses:
- Name the specific dish or detail the customer mentioned: "So glad you loved the short ribs — our chef has been fine-tuning that braising sauce for two years now…"
- Reveal a small treat: "Next time, ask the server about our off-menu special…"
- Invite with warmth: "Rainy days call for that soup — come in whenever, we'll have it warm and ready."

**4. Promotions: make customers feel "it's worth going now, not later"**

The core psychology of an in-store promotion is **time scarcity**: there's something special about going today that won't be there tomorrow. A good promotion must have: clear time window + specific, quantified benefit (not "great deals" but "second entrée at half price") + something worth sharing on social media.

**5. Every piece of content serves the single goal of getting someone through the door**

Whether it's menu copy, a review response, or a promotion plan — the final yardstick is always: did this sentence make someone more likely to walk in? Use that to judge every line.

## 🛠 How to do it

### Menu copy: four elements

```
① Dish name (keep the original name; an evocative subtitle is optional)
② Core sensory description (texture / aroma / temperature / appearance) — max 20 words
③ One scene anchor ("perfect on a cold day" / "always the first dish to disappear") — optional
④ Practical note (allergens / spice level / portion size) — where relevant
```

### Review response format templates

**Positive review responses (3 voice options to rotate):**

```
[Warm and personal]
Ha, so glad you called that out! [Specific dish name] is one of our most-asked-about things —
[brief detail about the dish or the chef]. Next visit, try [another dish] with it — they're
made for each other. Thanks for making our day, and come back soon. 🍜

[Quality-forward]
Thank you for noticing that detail. We'll pass your words about [dish name] directly to our
kitchen — positive feedback means more to them than you might think. Come back anytime and
let us know what else strikes you.

[Friendly / neighborhood]
This genuinely made our whole team smile! You came in on [day / occasion] and it sounds like
everything clicked — that's exactly what we're hoping to create every night.
Bring friends next time — the more the merrier.
```

**Negative review response (three-part template):**

```
Thank you for taking the time to share your experience.

What you described regarding [specific issue] clearly affected your visit, and we hear you.
[Explanation / corrective action: we have since / we are now / that evening was unusual because…
  — be honest, not defensive]

If you're open to giving us another chance, please come in and mention this to the host —
we'd like to make it right with [specific gesture]. Your feedback helps us improve,
and we appreciate your honesty.
```

### In-store promotion plan: five-piece framework

```
[Promotion name] One clear sentence — no cryptic labels
[Time window] [Month] [Day] – [Month] [Day] (specify days of week / time of day if limited)
[Core benefit] Exactly what the customer gets (specific number, not "great savings")
[How to participate] What the customer needs to do (say the code word / show a screenshot /
  spend over $X)
[Shareable element] Is there something worth photographing / posting about?
```

### Sample content pack (neighborhood burger joint scenario)

To give you a format reference:

```
=== Venue: The Corner Burger ===

[Menu copy samples — 3 dishes]

1. The Classic Smash (Signature)
   Original: two smashed beef patties, American cheese, house sauce
   Rewritten: The Classic Smash / crispy-edged, juicy in the middle — the kind of burger
   that makes you forget you planned to eat light today
   [Can be made single-patty on request]

2. Sweet Potato Fries (Side)
   Original: fresh-cut sweet potato, seasoned and fried
   Rewritten: Sweet Potato Fries / lightly crisp outside, fluffy inside, just sweet enough
   to hold you over while you wait for the main
   [Ask for the sriracha-honey dip — trust us]

3. Seasonal Shake (Dessert)
   Original: soft-serve base, seasonal fruit, house blends
   Rewritten: Seasonal Shake / thick enough that the straw stands up on its own —
   pick the flavor, photograph it, drink it

---

[Review response samples]

★ Positive response (customer mentioned the smash burger)
Right? That crust on the patty is everything. Our grill runs extra hot for exactly that reason.
Come back on a Tuesday — that's when we run our weekly off-menu special, and your kind of
palate will appreciate it. Thanks for the love! 🍔

★ Negative response (customer complained about a long wait)
Thank you for the honest feedback.

Waiting that long when you're hungry is genuinely not okay — we hear you. That night we were
short-staffed unexpectedly, which is no excuse but does explain it. We've since adjusted
our weekend prep to avoid the same bottleneck.

If you're willing to come back, ask for the manager when you arrive and mention your review —
we'd like to offer you our compliments on that visit. We want to earn back your trust.

---

[Promotion plan sample · Anniversary]

Promotion name: Corner Burger Turns 3 — The Regulars' Thank-You Week
Time window: [Saturday] – [Sunday next weekend], lunch and dinner service
Core benefit: Spend $25 or more, get a free Classic Smash (reg. $12)
How to participate: Show your Google review of The Corner Burger at the register
  (any rating, any time — just proof you've been here before)
Shareable element:
  · First 20 tables on Saturday get a "Year 3 badge" sticker — limited run, collectible
  · Post a photo tagging @thecornerburger and we'll share our favorites in our Story
  · A handwritten "thank you" board in the window with regulars' first names
    (collect names at the register this week — confirm with owner before displaying)
```
