## 📖 What You Will Learn

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to produce a complete **expert content package your principal can publish directly** — topic selection, a full-length answer or article body, and publishing recommendations, all bundled together.

First, let's talk about the atmosphere of these platforms.

Reddit, Quora, and Medium all share a common DNA: readers come here for **substance, not performance**. They are not looking for pretty photos or short dopamine hits — they want to actually learn something, or have a genuine question answered. The community holds a magnifying glass up to everything you post: Is that statistic real? Does the logic hold? Does this person actually know the field, or are they padding word count?

The underlying logic of content on these platforms is fundamentally different from, say, Instagram or TikTok:

- Instagram: "I tried this and it felt amazing" — emotion and aesthetics are the content
- Reddit / Quora / Medium: "Here is what is actually going on" — information density is the content

Your job is to help your principal write answers and articles that deliver **genuine information value** — something a reader finishes and thinks "I learned a specific thing I didn't know before," not "that was fine, I guess."

**Three main content formats (you need to distinguish between them):**

| Format | Best for | Length |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Expert Q&A answer | A specific question exists; your principal is an authority in the domain | 600–1,500 words |
| Long-form article (Medium publication / Reddit post) | Proactively publishing systematic knowledge or a unique perspective | 1,200–4,000 words |
| Short comment / thread | Quick takes, reacting to trending discussions | 100–400 words |

> This lesson focuses on **expert Q&A answers and long-form articles** — these are the core formats that build reputation and drive discovery on these platforms.

## 🧠 Core Principles (Internalize These)

**1. Lead with your conclusion, then give the argument**

This is the single most important rule for all three platforms. Do not write like an essay that builds to a reveal — readers are on their phones, and if the first paragraph has no clear payoff they will scroll past.

A good opening looks like: "The short answer is X. Here is why, in three parts —"

A bad opening looks like: "This is a great question that a lot of people have been asking. Let me start with some background context before we get into it..."

**2. Information increment earns upvotes**

The upvote logic on Reddit and Quora is: readers click the arrow when they feel "I learned something I couldn't have easily found elsewhere." So every answer or article must contain at least one thing that is genuinely hard to find elsewhere — your principal's firsthand experience, an industry insight, a counterintuitive conclusion, a detailed data comparison.

**3. Professional does not mean jargon-dense**

Many people misread what "expert content" means on these platforms. It does not mean using as many technical terms as possible. It means your reasoning holds up to scrutiny. Explaining a complex topic clearly in plain language is more respected than stuffing in terminology while the logic falls apart.

**4. Evergreen topics have long tails**

Content on Reddit, Quora, and Medium is searchable and indexed. A strong answer or article can be discovered a year after it was posted and keep driving traffic. When selecting topics, prioritize "evergreen questions" — the ones people will always search — over trending topics that burn out in a week.

**5. Draft is draft, publishing is publishing**

At no point should you log into any account, submit an answer, or hit the publish button. Your job ends at "hand the completed content package file to the principal."

## 🛠 Operational Details

### Two Routes to Topic Selection

**Route 1: Find high-traffic questions that already exist and write a better answer**

Reddit and Quora are full of questions that already have massive view counts — the traffic is built in. You just need to write a better answer than the existing top-voted ones. Selection criteria:
- The question has substantial engagement (many comments, upvotes, or views)
- The existing top answers have clear gaps (outdated, shallow, missing data, no firsthand perspective)
- Your principal genuinely has something useful to say on this topic

**Route 2: Proactively publish an original article**

Do not wait for someone else's question. Write the article your principal most wants to put out into the world. This works best when your principal has a distinctive methodology, a unique take on an industry, or an experience-based perspective that is not well represented online.

### Answer Structure Template (SCQA Framework)

Think of a Reddit or Quora answer like a **closing argument in a courtroom** — you give the verdict first (conclusion), then you present the evidence (argument), then you wrap up.

```
[Opening paragraph: Direct conclusion] (3–5 lines)
What does the person asking this question most want to know? Say it directly.
No wind-up, no teasing the answer.

[Body paragraphs: The argument]
Each major point gets its own paragraph, structured as:
  - Point sentence (one sentence, bolded)
  - Expansion: why is this true? what is the evidence?
  - Example or data: let the reader *see* it, not just hear about it

[Data / fact anchors] (woven into the argument)
At least 1–2 real data points or verifiable facts per piece
Format: "According to [source] / [study]..."
⚠️ Never fabricate statistics without a source. Write (source TBD) and let the principal fill it in.

[Address anticipated objections] (optional but adds credibility)
"Some people will say X — but actually..."
Preemptively handling likely pushback shows you have thought it through

[Closing]
One sentence that pulls the core point together, or gives the reader one action they can take immediately.
Do not end with "Does that answer your question?" or similar approval-seeking language.
```

### Formatting Guidelines

```
- Lead with the point: first paragraph should be no more than 5 lines
- Blank line between paragraphs (all three platforms support it — use it)
- Use bold subheadings to layer long answers
- Use numerals for numbers and data (50% not "fifty percent")
- Bold sparingly to highlight key terms (not everywhere)
- Minimal emoji — Reddit and Quora expect professional tone; use sparingly if at all
- Tables work well for comparisons (all three platforms render them)
```

### Topic Quality Self-Check

After drafting, go through this checklist:

```
□ Does the first paragraph contain a clear conclusion or stance?
□ Is there at least one point the reader genuinely cannot find easily elsewhere?
□ Are all data points sourced, or marked (source TBD)?
□ Does the argument hold up against likely pushback?
□ Has the AI-voice been removed — does it read like a real person talking?
□ Does the title contain clear keywords so it can be found via search?
```

### Title Optimization

Good titles for Reddit posts, Quora answers (question titles), and Medium articles need to do two things: make people want to click, and be findable through search.

| Type | Formula | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Direct conclusion | [Conclusion], because [N reasons] | Do AI writing tools actually work? Yes — but only for 80% of the repetitive parts |
| Deep dive | What is really behind [phenomenon]? | Why do the most upvoted answers on Quora keep reading like ads? |
| Practical guide | How to [do X] (complete walkthrough) | How I used AI to build a content system — and the 5 mistakes I made |
| Counterintuitive | Most people assume [X], but the truth is [Y] | Most people think long-form always wins on Medium — here is what actually matters |
