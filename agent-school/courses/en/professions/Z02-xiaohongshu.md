## 📖 What you'll learn

After this lesson, you will deliver a **ready-to-use Instagram content pack** — a viral-post draft plus a visual card plan, bundled together.

First, let's talk about Instagram's "terrain." If a newsletter is like a magazine, Instagram is more like **a friend recommending something to you over coffee**. It's not a lecture — it's a share. Not "here's what you should do," but "I tried this, it's amazing, look." That atmosphere shapes the writing: it needs to feel real, warm, and make the reader think "this is speaking directly to me."

Your job is to make the owner's content feel like **something a real Instagram user wrote**, not "promotional copy written by AI for a brand."

**On ready-made tools (research first, ask before installing):**

- **Canva**: Create Instagram-native image cards (4:5 portrait, 1:1 square) with hundreds of templates. Free tier is sufficient for most use cases. [canva.com](https://canva.com)
- **Later / Buffer**: Schedule and publish Instagram posts; also provide best-time-to-post analytics. [later.com](https://later.com) · [buffer.com](https://buffer.com)
- **social-card generators**: Various open-source tools exist for generating styled text cards (search GitHub for "social card generator"). Evaluate and recommend to the owner.

> ⚠️ Research is research — **installation requires the owner's explicit confirmation first**. This lesson's focus is producing content drafts; you must never log in to or operate any real account.

## 🧠 Core principles (internalize these as habits)

**1. The Instagram "three-move combo": hook + pain point + recommendation**

Imagine walking into a store where the shelves are packed with posts. Readers' thumbs scroll past each one in **under a second**. Instagram's traffic logic is that ruthless.

The three-move combo is non-negotiable:
- **Lead with a visual hook in the image or first line**: it's not decoration — it's a "signal flag" that helps readers spot your category in the feed. For example: ☕ lifestyle, 💼 career, ✨ beauty finds.
- **Hit a pain point**: the first 1–2 lines must make the reader feel "this is talking about me," e.g. "Stayed up until 3 AM redoing that presentation — then found out AI could've done it in five minutes??"
- **Recommend, don't advertise**: say "after I used XX…" not "XX product features include…". Experience beats specs.

**2. Instagram is viewed on a vertical phone screen**

That means: break paragraphs every 2–3 lines, use line breaks instead of long paragraphs, use portrait images (4:5) rather than landscape, and drop emojis into the body as visual dividers.

**3. Comments are part of the content**

High-quality posts end with an "engagement hook" — something like "Has this ever happened to you? Tell me in the comments 💬". This isn't filler; it's a signal that tells the algorithm to push your post to more people.

**4. Draft is draft, publish is publish**

Same iron rule as Z01: your job ends at "hand the content pack to the owner." Log in, post, operate the account — those are all the owner's moves, never yours.

## 🛠 How to do it

### Structure of a high-performing Instagram post

Think of the Instagram post as a **sandwich**:

```
[Top bread]  Caption hook: emoji + pain point / intrigue / benefit, ≤125 characters
             (this is the only text visible before "more")
[Filling]    Opening: first 2 lines — hook — either hit pain point or deliver surprise
             Middle: 3–5 "reasons to love this" or steps, each as a short line + line break
             Image notes: what each image/slide covers
             Engagement hook: one question that invites comments
[Bottom bread] Hashtags: #relevant-tags × 5–10 (mix large + niche)
```

### The content pack five-piece set (assemble all five every time)

```
[Caption options]     3 options, each leading with an emoji, different styles
                      (pain-point / benefit / curiosity)
[Content positioning] Target audience / reason for this topic / suggested posting window
[Caption draft]
  - Opening hook (≤2 lines, stops the scroll)
  - Body (2–3 lines per paragraph, line breaks, emojis as dividers)
  - Closing engagement hook
  - Hashtag suggestions (5–10 tags)
[Visual card plan]    How many images/slides / what goes on each / style recommendation
[Self-check verdict]  Does it read like a real user sharing something, or like an ad?
```

### Caption formula cheat sheet

| Type | Formula | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Pain-point | emoji + pain scenario + "??" | ✨ Redesigning that deck at midnight for the third time?? |
| Benefit | emoji + concrete gain + audience | 💼 For anyone with a 9–5: AI just gave me back 2 hours a day |
| Curiosity | emoji + counter-intuitive + reveal tease | 🤯 I thought AI-written content was obvious — until I saw this |
| Tutorial | emoji + step feel + outcome | 📱 How I made an Instagram post with AI in under 10 minutes |

### Visual card notes

Instagram-native sizes: **4:5 portrait** for feed posts, **9:16 vertical** for Stories/Reels, **1:1 square** also works for feed.

When spec-ing a card, provide:
- Main text (headline or core idea)
- Sub-text (supporting line or source)
- Style direction (minimal / gradient / dark / handwritten feel)

> ⚠️ Check whether the owner has a preferred design tool before recommending one. If nothing is installed, write "suggest creating card with Canva — template style: [X]" in the content pack.

### Hashtag strategy

Mix principle: 2–3 large-volume tags (1M+ posts) + 3–4 niche tags (100K range) + 1–2 hyper-specific community tags.

Example (AI tools post):
```
#AItools #productivityhacks #aiwriting #instagramgrowth #contentcreation
#creatortips #worksmarter #techforeveryone
```

**You must:**
