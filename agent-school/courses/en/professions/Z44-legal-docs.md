# Lesson Z44 · Legal Document Templates

> **Student: you, the AI agent currently enrolled** ｜ Prerequisites: Lessons J01–J07 ｜ Difficulty: ★★☆ ｜ Source: general legal-document drafting principles; jurisdiction-neutral templates — applicable statutes vary by country, always cite "[applicable statute]" or the real statute for the owner's jurisdiction; this is not legal advice, consult a qualified lawyer before use

## 📖 What you'll learn

After this lesson, you will help the owner draft **templates for common commercial legal documents** — infringement notice letters, copyright statements, disclaimers, memoranda of understanding — giving the owner a structurally correct, well-worded draft that a lawyer can "review and send" rather than build from scratch.

**Important disclaimer (required reading — pass this on to the owner):**

> ⚠️ **AI-generated legal document drafts are for reference only, do not constitute legal advice, and have no formal legal effect on their own.** The enforceability of a legal document depends on specific facts, applicable law, and local practice. **Please have any document reviewed and confirmed by a qualified lawyer before formal use.**

Think of yourself as a skilled document assistant at a law firm. A lawyer has sketched out the general idea, and your job is to fill in the blank template — get the format right, include all required elements, use appropriate formal language — and hand it back to the lawyer for a quick review and a few tweaks before it goes out. You're not the lawyer, but you save the lawyer half the time. That's the skill this lesson builds.

## 🧠 Core principles (internalize these as habits)

1. **Legal documents have a fixed skeleton — don't rearrange it.** Each type of document has a conventional structure: title, sender, recipient, body, signature block, date. Just as a formal leave-of-absence request must include "who is requesting / reason / dates / supervisor signature," a legal document's elements are non-negotiable. Missing elements weaken the document's effect.

2. **State the facts clearly and specifically — no vagueness.** "The other party infringed" is not enough — you need "the other party, on [date], on [platform], in the form of [action], infringed our [specific right] under [applicable statute, section X]." The more specific the facts, the more weight the document carries.

3. **State your demand clearly and give the other party a reasonable deadline.** A notice letter that just says "you infringed, please take note" can be safely ignored. Write: "Please take the following actions within [X] business days of receiving this letter: …" This is both good practice and, in many jurisdictions, a procedural prerequisite before escalation.

4. **Keep the tone formal; remove emotion.** A legal document is not a complaint letter. Don't write "your conduct is outrageous." Write: "The above conduct constitutes an infringement of [right] under [applicable statute]. [Our company / I] reserve all rights to seek remedies in accordance with applicable law." Formal, restrained, supported by stated authority.

5. **Mark every document "Draft" and include a disclaimer.** In the header or footer: "This document draft was generated with AI assistance. Please have it reviewed by a qualified lawyer before formal use."

## 🛠 How to do it

### Common document types at a glance

| Document type | Typical use case | Core elements |
|---------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Infringement notice letter | Counterparty copied content / imitated a trademark / used images without authorization | Infringement facts + legal basis + specific demand + deadline |
| Copyright statement | Website / app / publication copyright notice | Rights ownership + prohibited acts + licensing terms + contact |
| Disclaimer | Limiting liability for a product / service / content | Scope of service + no-warranty language + liability limitation + governing law |
| Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) | Confirming intent at the start of a partnership | Party details + scope of cooperation + rights and duties + confidentiality + validity period |
| Authorization / License letter | Granting another party the right to use content / images / code | Licensor / licensee + licensed content + scope of use + term + fees |

### Infringement notice letter — standard template framework

```
[Document title]
Notice Regarding Infringement of [Our / My] [Copyright / Trademark / Other Right]

To: [Recipient name]

I. Sender information
Sender: [Company name / Individual name]
Address: [Contact address]
Contact: [Email / Phone]

II. Infringement facts
[Recipient] on [date] at / on [platform / channel / URL] published / used [describe the
infringing content].
The above content is original [type of work] created / published by [us / me] on [date],
hereinafter referred to as the "Work." [We / I] hold the [copyright / trademark / other right]
to the Work and retain complete evidence.

III. Legal basis
Pursuant to [applicable statute, section X — insert the real statute for the owner's
jurisdiction, e.g., the Copyright Act, the Intellectual Property Code, or write
"[applicable intellectual property statute]"], the above conduct constitutes an infringement
of [our / my] rights.

IV. Demand
Please take the following actions within [10] business days of receiving this letter:
1. Immediately cease the above infringing conduct;
2. Remove / take down the infringing content;
3. Provide a written apology to [us / me] for the infringement;
4. Compensate [us / me] for losses of [amount or "an amount to be negotiated"].

Failure to respond or act within the above period may result in [us / me] filing a complaint
with the relevant authorities or initiating legal proceedings, at which point all legal
consequences and costs will be borne by [you / your organization].

V. Signature block
Sender: [Signature / Company seal]
Date: [Day Month Year]

---
⚠️ This draft was generated with AI assistance for reference only. Please have it reviewed
by a qualified lawyer before sending.
```

### Disclaimer — standard template framework

```
[Document title]
Disclaimer — [Product / Service / Website Name]

1. Accuracy of information
The information provided through this [product / platform / content] is for reference only.
[We] make every effort to ensure accuracy, but make no express or implied warranty regarding
the completeness, accuracy, or timeliness of the information. Users should independently
verify relevant information.

2. Limitations on service
This [product / service] does not provide [medical / legal / financial / investment / other]
advice. The content on this platform does not constitute professional advice. Users should
consult a qualified professional in the relevant field before making any significant decision.

3. Limitation of liability
To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, [we] shall not be liable for any direct,
indirect, incidental, or consequential loss arising from the use of this [product / service /
content].

4. Third-party links
This platform may contain links to third-party websites or services. [We] assume no
responsibility for third-party content, privacy practices, or conduct.

5. Governing law and disputes
This disclaimer is governed by [applicable law — insert jurisdiction, or write "[governing
law of the owner's jurisdiction]"]. Disputes shall be resolved by [applicable dispute
resolution mechanism].

6. Updates
This disclaimer may be updated periodically. Updates will be announced in a prominent
location on this platform. Continued use constitutes acceptance of the updated disclaimer.

Effective date: [Day Month Year]
---
⚠️ This draft was generated with AI assistance for reference only. Please have it reviewed
by a qualified lawyer before formal use.
```

### Four-step drafting workflow

```
Step 1: Confirm the document type
  → Ask the owner: who is this being sent to? What problem does it solve?
    What do you want the other party to do?

Step 2: Gather key information
  → Sender details (company name / individual name / contact)
  → Recipient details (name / contact)
  → Statement of facts (when / where / description of conduct)
  → Legal basis (which rule applies — insert "[applicable statute]" if unknown)
  → Demand (what to do / what deadline)

Step 3: Fill in the template
  → Work through the corresponding template section by section
  → No core element may be omitted
  → The facts section must be specific and verifiable

Step 4: Mark and deliver
  → Label the document "Draft v1.0" at the top
  → Use [brackets] for items requiring the owner's confirmation or
    a lawyer's input
  → Add disclaimer at the bottom
```
