## 📖 What You Will Walk Away With

By the end of this lesson you will deliver a **complete owned-audience content pack your owner can run with immediately** — not a lecture on "why you should build an audience," but an actual 7-day email/feed content calendar, a community SOP card, 1-on-1 outreach scripts, and a subscriber segmentation guide, packaged and ready to hand off.

Picture yourself as a **community steward**. You're looking after an email list and Discord server with 500 real people. Those people will go quiet, get annoyed, and hit unsubscribe — your job is to make them feel "this community is worth staying in," so that when the moment is right, they buy on their own initiative. You are not a bot blasting ads. You are **tending a real relationship**.

**Iron rule: your job is to produce content drafts. Hitting send — on email, Discord, or anything else — is the owner's call, not yours.**

## 🧠 Core Principles

### 1. An Owned Audience Is a Relationship Bank Account

Every time you deposit something valuable — useful tips, genuine company, an exclusive deal, a pleasant surprise — withdrawing later (asking people to buy, refer a friend) gets easier. **Lead with promotions and you overdraft the account.** In healthy owned-audience operations, promotional content rarely exceeds 30% of total output; the other 70% is value and relationship-building.

### 2. Newsletter = Storefront Window · Community = Town Square · 1-on-1 = Coffee Chat

- **Email newsletter / social feed**: the storefront window people scroll past every day — keep it calm and interesting. One to two pieces of content per day, visual and warm, leaving readers thinking "I like what this brand/person is about."
- **Discord server / Slack workspace**: the bustling town square — it needs energy and conversation threads. Daily topics, designed interactions, value drops.
- **Personalized email / CRM outreach**: the most intimate coffee chat, the only place where real purchase decisions actually move forward. Never blast. Be warm. Remember the person.

### 3. SOPs Exist So the Executor Never Has to Guess

A great community SOP is like a restaurant service manual — a brand-new team member can pick it up on day one and know exactly what to do without asking "what should I say right now?" Whatever SOP you write, a first-week community manager should be able to follow it without going off-script.

### 4. Segmentation Is the Foundation of Personalized Operations

Not every subscriber gets the same treatment. At minimum, sort into three tiers:

- **Prospects**: just joined, trust not yet established — lead with value, hold the sales pitch
- **Warm leads**: have engaged, asked questions — follow up, answer objections, give them a gentle nudge
- **Existing customers**: have already bought — nurture the relationship, drive repeat purchases and referrals

## 🛠 How to Do It

### Standard Structure of an Owned-Audience Content Pack

```
Owned-Audience Content Pack (ready for owner to use):
├── 1-email-feed-content-calendar.md    7-day or 30-day plan, 1–2 posts/emails per day
├── 2-community-sop-card.md             Discord/Slack daily & weekly cadence, topic library
├── 3-1on1-outreach-scripts.md          Scene-by-scene scripts: welcome / follow-up / close / re-engage
└── 4-subscriber-segmentation-guide.md  Criteria & actions for Prospect / Warm Lead / Customer tiers
```

### Five Content Types for Your Email/Feed Calendar (rotate every week)

| Type | Frequency | Purpose | Trigger phrases |
|------|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| Value tip / insight | 2–3× per week | Build authority | "Here's something I learned today…" / "An industry truth nobody talks about…" |
| Personal / behind-the-scenes | 1–2× per week | Build authenticity | Day-in-the-life, small genuine moments |
| Customer story | 1–2× per week | Build trust | "A subscriber told me this week…" |
| Product recommendation | 1–2× per week | Drive sales | Scene-based description, no hard sell |
| Poll / Q&A | 1× per week | Boost engagement | "Which do you prefer — A or B?" |

### Sample Copy (Skincare Brand Email List)

**Email newsletter · Value tip type**

> Oily skin and breakouts every time the season changes — it's almost never an "oily skin" problem. It's actually a **dehydration** problem.
>
> When your skin is too dry, your body overcompensates by pumping out extra oil. Washing your face more often or loading up on oil-control products just strips more moisture away — and kicks the cycle into overdrive.
>
> The fix? Use a good moisturizer morning and night. Give your skin water first, and the excess oil production calms down on its own.
>
> What moisturizer are you using right now? Hit reply and let me know — I read every response. 👇

---

**Email newsletter · Product recommendation type (compliant)**

> Restocked my favorite moisturizer last week — grabbed two this time. One for me, one for my mom.
>
> Three months in, the change I notice most: my face doesn't feel tight after washing it in the morning. That used to be a real problem for me every fall and winter, no matter what I tried.
>
> Not trying to push this on you — I just find it's genuinely good, and that's the only reason I'm mentioning it.
> If you're curious, just reply to this email. Happy to share details.

---

**Discord server · Engagement topic (post Wednesday afternoon)**

> 🎯 **This week's community topic:**
> Anyone else noticing seasonal skin changes lately?
> Drop your "seasonal skin struggle" below — first person to share gets a free sample pack sent their way 👇
> (Tag a friend who has the same problem and they're entered too!)

---

**1-on-1 welcome email (sent within 5 minutes of someone joining the list or server)**

> Hey, welcome! I'm [Name] — this community is all about sharing skincare tips and the occasional exclusive deal for members.
>
> From time to time I share finds I genuinely love with the community first, before anyone else. 😊
>
> Quick question: what's your main skin concern right now? I'll point you to the two or three posts that are most relevant for you specifically.

---

**1-on-1 follow-up (asked about price but didn't buy — 3 days later)**

> Hey! You asked about [product] the other day — just wanted to check in and see if you found something that works for you.
>
> I just restocked, and if you're still looking I'm happy to set one aside for you.
> And if you had any questions you didn't get to ask, just shoot them over — I'm always happy to help. 😊

---

**1-on-1 re-engagement (past customer, inactive for 3 months)**

> It's been a while — hope you're doing well!
> How did [product] work out for you? I'd love to hear.
>
> I'm running a loyalty appreciation thing right now for people who've been with me for a while, and you were the first person I thought of.
> Let me know how things are going with your skin and I'll see if there's something that fits where you're at now.

### Community SOP Daily Cadence

```
Daily must-dos (team member follows this checklist):
[Mon–Fri 9:00 AM]  Morning greeting + one value tip (image + text)
[Mon–Fri 12:30 PM] Lunchtime topic (light, low-pressure conversation)
[Mon–Fri 8:00 PM]  Evening push (product info / event / customer story — max 3× per week)

Weekly recurring actions:
[Monday]    Weekly preview (what's coming this week in the community)
[Wednesday] Deep-dive interaction (trivia with a prize / poll)
[Friday]    Weekend perk (free sample / discount code / content resource)
[Sunday]    Customer story spotlight (real feedback, anonymized if needed)

Monthly recurring actions:
[Start of month]   Shout out top-engaged members from last month
[Mid-month]        Member appreciation day or limited-time event
[End of month]     Monthly wrap-up + next-month preview
```
