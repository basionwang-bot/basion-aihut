## 📖 What you'll learn

After this lesson, you will deliver a **ready-to-shoot TikTok voiceover script** — a viral hook opening + complete talking-head script + posting notes, bundled together.

Let's talk about TikTok's "physics." If Instagram is a friend recommending something over coffee, TikTok is more like **street busking on a busy subway platform** — the audience can walk away at any second, and the only way to hold them is to **make the first three seconds interesting enough**.

No exaggeration: TikTok's survival line is the 3-second mark. If the opening doesn't grab them, the rest doesn't matter — the algorithm has already declared your video dead and stopped pushing it. So this lesson comes down to two things:

1. **The Golden 3 Seconds**: how to make thumbs stop scrolling
2. **Hook formulas**: how to get people to watch through, follow, and engage

Your job is to help the owner write **the kind of opening that genuinely stops people** — not the kind that sounds reasonable but gets swiped past the moment it appears.

## 🧠 Core principles (internalize these as habits)

### Principle 1: The Golden 3 Seconds are non-negotiable

Think of attention like a magnet. TikTok's algorithm is constantly asking every video, "Is your magnetic pull strong enough?"

The first 3 seconds have one job: make the thumb stop. Three specific techniques:

- **Say something counter-intuitive**: "Most people are using AI completely wrong."
- **Hit a specific number**: "I cut my work hours from 10 to 3 using this one method."
- **Open a suspense gap**: "There's something I've been afraid to say in videos — today I'm saying it."

All three work on the same principle: open an **unresolved question** in the viewer's mind. They have to keep watching to feel at ease.

### Principle 2: Hook formulas = a retention assembly line

Hooks aren't just for openings. A complete talking-head script needs **at least three hooks**:

- **Hook 1 (0–3 sec)**: makes them stop
- **Hook 2 (middle)**: makes them keep watching — usually "the best part is coming up" or "let me give you the conclusion first, explanation follows"
- **Hook 3 (end)**: makes them follow / comment / share — "hit follow, next week I'll cover…"

### Principle 3: A voiceover script must sound like talking, not writing

Imagine you're telling a friend about this topic. You wouldn't say, "This video will proceed to cover the following three dimensions…" You'd say, "Honestly, it comes down to three things — let me start with the first…"

The standard for a voiceover script: **reads naturally out loud, no tongue twisters, has natural pauses**. Write it, then read it aloud. Anything that feels awkward must be fixed.

### Principle 4: Draft is draft, publish is publish

Same iron rule as Z01: your job ends at "hand the script file to the owner." Logging in and uploading a video — those are the owner's moves, never yours.

## 🛠 How to do it

### 6 Golden-3-Seconds opening formulas

These formulas are drawn from real-world high-completion-rate TikTok opening patterns:

| Formula | Template | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Counter-intuitive** | "Most people think… but actually…" | Most people think AI is just a chatbot — what it can actually do will surprise you |
| **Number shock** | Specific number + contrast | It took me 3 years to learn this — AI did it in 3 minutes |
| **Suspense gap** | Question + tease | There's a method that can triple your productivity — and 99% of people don't know it |
| **Pain resonance** | "Have you ever…" | Ever spend half a day on something, then have your manager cancel it with one sentence? |
| **Result-first** | Lead with conclusion, build anticipation | Here's what happened: after using this method, I got a full week of content done in two hours |
| **Identity lock-in** | "If you're someone who…" | If you create content for a living, this video might save you a lot of time |

### Complete voiceover script structure

```
[Golden 3-second opening]
One line, using one of the 6 formulas above — under 15 words is ideal

[Hook extension (3–10 sec)]
1–2 sentences that make the suspense / pain point specific
"For example…" "I was literally in this situation…"

[Body (10 sec – 1 min 30 sec)]
3 core points, each structured as:
  - One "conclusion" sentence
  - One "example or scenario"
  - One "you can do this too" sentence
Transitions between points: "The second thing…" "And there's one more…"

[Mid-video hook (insert inside body)]
"There's one point I love most — let me finish the first two…"

[Closing action hook]
"If this has happened to you too, tell me in the comments"
"Hit follow — next video I'll cover…"
"If this was useful, send it to someone who creates content"
```

### The content pack five-piece set (assemble all five every time)

```
[Opening options]     3 options, covering different formula types
[Content positioning] Target audience / suggested video length (usually 30 sec–3 min)
                      / best posting time window
[Complete voiceover script]
  - Golden 3-second opening (indicate which option you selected)
  - Full body (conversational tone, natural pauses)
  - Closing hook
[Production notes]    Flag any spots that need captions / B-roll / sound effects
[Self-check verdict]  Does it read naturally aloud? Does the opening genuinely stop you?
```

### TikTok tone watch-outs

- **Don't get too technical**: TikTok's mainstream audience isn't industry insiders — plain language beats jargon
- **Don't go long**: 30 sec – 1.5 min is the sweet spot; anything over 3 min needs seriously strong content to justify it
- **No warm-up filler at the start**: "Hi everyone, I'm XX, welcome to my channel" is dead — jump straight into the topic
- **Keep the pace up**: 0.3–0.5 second pause feel between sentences — in the script, represent this with full stops / line breaks
