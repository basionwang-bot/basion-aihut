## 📖 What you'll learn

By the end of this lesson, you will have helped your owner build a **ready-to-use customer service FAQ script library** — with questions properly categorized, standard responses written, and tone guidelines set — packaged into a file the support team can pick up and use immediately.

First, let's talk about why this matters.

Picture this: a company with five support agents fielding a flood of repetitive questions every day. "How long does shipping take?" "How do I request a refund?" "Is this product right for my situation?" — the same questions, dozens of times a day.

What happens? Five people answer the same question five different ways. One says "3–5 business days," another says "as fast as 3 days," a third leaves it vague — and the customer is left confused. On top of that, every agent is spending mental energy re-solving problems that have already been solved.

A good FAQ script library fixes exactly this: it turns "repetitive thinking" into "look it up."

Think of it like a restaurant writing a *Guest FAQ Handbook*: Chapter 1 covers menu questions, Chapter 2 covers the dining environment, Chapter 3 covers complaints. A server just flips to the right page — no reinventing the wheel every time. Your job is to write that handbook.

**What you produce is something the support team can use directly — with one condition: the owner must review and verify all the business-specific details.** You are providing the framework and sample text; the owner needs to fill in the real numbers, policies, and processes.

## 🧠 Core principles (internalize these as habits)

**1. Organize categories from the customer's point of view**

Don't sort by internal department ("Logistics questions" / "Finance questions"). Sort by the type of question the customer has. Customers don't know how your company is structured — they only know what their problem is.

A solid category structure looks like this:
- Pre-sales inquiries (Is this product right for me? How is pricing calculated?)
- Ordering / payment (How do I place an order? What if my payment fails?)
- Shipping / delivery (When will it ship? How do I track it?)
- After-sales support (How do I return or exchange? Can I get a receipt?)
- Complaints and feedback (Something went wrong — what now?)
- Account / membership (How do I sign up? I forgot my password.)

**2. Four elements of a strong response: Acknowledge + Answer + Action + Care**

A good support reply isn't a cold, robotic "The answer is: X." Here's the structure that works:

```
Acknowledge:  Let the customer feel heard first
Answer:       Give a clear, direct response (be specific — use numbers, steps)
Action:       If the customer needs to do something, tell them exactly what's next
Care:         Close with a warm sentence — don't just cut off abruptly
```

Take "shipping time" as an example:

❌ Weak version: "Ships in 3–5 business days."

✓ Strong version: "Hi there! Once your order is placed, we'll arrange shipment within 1 business day, and you can expect delivery in 3–5 business days. You can track your order's progress in real time on the order details page. Don't hesitate to reach out if you need anything — happy shopping!"

**3. Keep the tone consistent and true to the owner's brand voice**

Some brands are warm and playful (consumer products aimed at younger audiences). Others are polished and authoritative (B2B or financial products). Some are light and concise. Before you write a single line, ask the owner: "What feeling do you want customers to have when they talk to you?" If there's no clear answer, look at their existing support history or marketing content and make a judgment call.

**4. Always include an "escalation path" for edge cases**

Not every situation can be handled with a script. The FAQ library should clearly flag which scenarios require a handoff to a human agent: disputes involving money, emotionally escalated customers, issues outside the normal range, threats to go to the press, and so on.

**5. A script library is a living document — keep it updated**

Products change, policies evolve, new high-frequency questions emerge. The FAQ library needs to keep pace. Put a "Last updated" date at the top of the document as a reminder to the owner to maintain it regularly.

## 🛠 How to do it

### Step 1: Gather raw material

Before writing a single script, ask the owner for as much of the following as they can share:

```
□ Past support chat logs (anonymized) — mine these for high-frequency questions
□ Ticket category stats from your support system — which issues come up most
□ Any existing FAQ page on the product or service — don't reinvent what's already there
□ Actual policy documents for refunds, shipping, and after-sales — your source of truth for the numbers
```

If the owner has no historical data, use this interview guide to help them surface the most common questions:

```
[Interview guide for surfacing high-frequency questions]
1. What are the top 5 questions customers most often email or chat in about?
2. What questions are hardest for agents to answer, or most often answered incorrectly?
3. What types of questions most often leave customers frustrated or dissatisfied?
4. Have any new high-frequency questions appeared in the last three months?
5. Which questions require escalation to a manager or another team to resolve?
```

### Step 2: FAQ script library template (reuse directly)

Below is a complete sample template suitable for a typical e-commerce or service business:

---

```markdown
# [Company Name] Customer Service Script Library
**Last updated: YYYY-MM-DD**
**Applicable channels: email / live chat / Zendesk / phone**
**Tone: warm and professional (replace with actual brand voice)**

---

## 1. Universal openers

**First contact:**
Hi there! Thanks for reaching out to [Company Name] support. I'm [Name/Agent ID], and I'm happy to help. What can I do for you today?

**While the customer is waiting:**
Hi! Thanks for your patience — I'm on it. Give me just a moment.

**Closing:**
Thanks so much for reaching out! If anything else comes up, we're always here. Hope you have a great day!

---

## 2. Pre-sales inquiries

**Q: Is this product right for [a specific type of person/situation]?**
> A: Hi! [Product name] is designed for [target audience description]. [Briefly explain when it's a good fit — and when it might not be.] If you're still not sure whether it's right for you, tell me a bit about your situation and I'll help you figure it out!

**Q: How much does it cost? Are there any discounts?**
> A: Hi! [Product name] is currently priced at [specific price]. [Describe any active promotions, if applicable.] If you're looking at a larger purchase, feel free to ask about volume pricing — we'd love to work something out!

**Q: How does this compare to [competitor product]?**
> A: Great question! Our product stands out in [key differentiator 1] and [key differentiator 2]. [Honestly describe the best-fit scenario.] If you tell me what matters most to you, I can give you a more targeted comparison.

---

## 3. Ordering / payment

**Q: How do I place an order?**
> A: It's simple! [Specific steps, listed 1–2–3.] If you run into any trouble along the way, feel free to send me a screenshot — I'll walk you through it.

**Q: My payment failed. What do I do?**
> A: Sorry about the trouble! Payment failures usually come down to a few things: [list 2–3 common causes, e.g., insufficient funds / network issue / bank-side limit]. Try [specific steps]. If it's still not working, send me a screenshot and I'll loop in our technical team to sort it out!

**Q: Can I get a receipt or proof of purchase?**
> A: Absolutely! We can provide [electronic receipt / invoice / proof of purchase]. Please share [the details needed — name, address, email, etc.], and we'll send it to your inbox within [X business days] of your order being fulfilled.

---

## 4. Shipping / delivery

**Q: When will my order ship?**
> A: Hi! Orders are dispatched within [X business days] of purchase (public holidays may add a day or two), and estimated delivery is [X–X business days] after that. You can track your shipment in real time at [tracking link/page].

**Q: Why hasn't my order shipped yet?**
> A: So sorry for the wait! Let me pull up your order. Your current status is [status], and it's expected to ship by [date/time]. If it hasn't moved within [X days], I'll flag it for priority handling!

**Q: My tracking hasn't updated / it's been stuck for a while.**
> A: I completely understand the concern! Tracking can stall for a few reasons: [reason 1: scan delay in transit / reason 2: in transfer hub / reason 3: weather or other delays]. Let me reach out to the carrier to get a status update. Could you leave your preferred contact info? I'll get back to you as soon as I hear something.

---

## 5. After-sales support

**Q: How do I return or get a refund?**
> A: It's easy to get started! [Return policy: e.g., X-day no-questions-asked window / applicable conditions.] Just [steps: e.g., click "Request Return" on your order page], and we'll process it within [X business days]. The refund goes back to your original payment method — keep an eye out for it.

**Q: My item arrived damaged or defective. What happens now?**
> A: I'm really sorry about this — that's not the experience we want for you. Could you send a few photos (full item, damaged area, and packaging)? I'll get a replacement, re-shipment, or refund sorted for you right away — no need to send anything back.

**Q: I'm past the return window. Can anything still be done?**
> A: I hear you — let's see what we can do. Requests outside the standard window go through a special review process. Can you walk me through what happened? If there's a way to make it right, I'll do my best to find it.

---

## 6. Complaints and escalations

**Q: I want to make a complaint / customer is upset**
> A: **[Guiding principle: calm the situation first, then solve the problem]**
>
> Hi — I completely hear you. You've experienced [restate the customer's issue], and that's genuinely frustrating. I understand, and it's something we need to do better.
>
> [If the issue is straightforward and can be resolved immediately]: Let me take care of [specific solution] for you right now — I'll have it sorted today.
>
> [If it's more complex and needs time]: I need to bring in [team/supervisor] to handle this properly. I'll have an update for you within [timeframe] — does that work for you?
>
> **⚠️ Escalation triggers (hand off to a human agent):**
> - Customer is requesting compensation above [threshold amount]
> - Customer mentions escalating to media, consumer protection authorities, or regulatory bodies
> - Issue involves personal safety or product safety concerns
> - Customer insists on speaking directly with a manager or decision-maker

---

## 7. Account / membership

**Q: How do I sign up or log in?**
> A: [Specific sign-up steps.] If you're not receiving the verification code, double-check that [phone number / email is entered correctly], check your spam folder, or hit "Resend." Still stuck? I'm right here.

**Q: I forgot my password. How do I reset it?**
> A: No worries! Click [Forgot password] on the login page, enter your registered email or phone number, and follow the code you receive to set a new password. Once you're in, it's worth saving it somewhere safe for easy access later.
```
