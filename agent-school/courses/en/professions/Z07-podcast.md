## 📖 What you'll learn

After this lesson, you will deliver a **complete podcast content pack the owner can plug in as soon as recording is done** — episode planning + voiceover script + ready-to-publish show notes, bundled together.

First, let's understand podcasts as a medium. Unlike a blog post you can scroll through non-linearly, and unlike a short video that lives or dies by a 3-second hook — a podcast is more like **a friend talking to you on a commute**. The listener is doing something else with their eyes and hands while their ears are open. That means: content can't be too mentally demanding, the thread must be extremely clear, and there should be no long stretches of "you can skip this."

Your core job has two parts:

One: write a script that **sounds like talking, not reading** — they're two different animals. A written sentence can have complex subordinate clauses and long logical arguments. A spoken sentence needs short phrases, pause markers, and the occasional "so basically…" and "here's the thing…"

Two: write **show notes that are actually useful** — not a copy-paste of the script, but a distilled, searchable resource: timestamps, guest info, tools / books / links mentioned in the episode. Let someone who hasn't listened understand at a glance whether this episode is worth their time; let someone who did listen find that thing you mentioned in three seconds.

**On ready-made tools (research first, ask before installing):**

- **Whisper (local audio transcription)**: Converts recorded audio files into text transcripts — the raw material for writing show notes. Runs entirely locally, no data uploaded to any cloud, privacy-friendly. [github.com/openai/whisper](https://github.com/openai/whisper)
- **humanizer (de-AI-ify)**: Run the script through this after writing to remove AI cadence and make it sound more naturally spoken. [github.com/blader/humanizer](https://github.com/blader/humanizer)
- **Spotify for Podcasters**: The dominant English-language podcast distribution platform; sets the standard for show notes, cover art, and episode metadata. [podcasters.spotify.com](https://podcasters.spotify.com)
- **Apple Podcasts Connect**: The second major distribution channel; submit RSS feed or use Spotify's distribution option. [podcastsconnect.apple.com](https://podcastsconnect.apple.com)

> ⚠️ Whisper and similar tools: research is research — **installation requires the owner's explicit confirmation first**. This lesson focuses on producing content drafts; you must never log in to any account or actually upload / publish any podcast files.

## 🧠 Core principles (internalize these as habits)

**1. A script is "spoken writing," not "written writing"**

The most common mistake is taking a blog post, tweaking it, and calling it a script. A perfectly normal written sentence is: "Based on the analysis above, we can conclude that the core moat of content entrepreneurship lies in…" — try reading that aloud. It sounds like a recitation, not a conversation.

The spoken version should be: "So you see, what makes content entrepreneurship hardest? Honestly, it comes down to…"

The trick: after writing the script, silently "speak" it in your head — if it flows out naturally, it's done. If it doesn't, rewrite it.

**2. Every episode needs "one clear thing to take home"**

A test for podcast episode topics: after listening, what's the one sentence the listener could repeat to a friend? If they can't summarize it, the topic is too scattered. Good episodes create sentences like: "I didn't know that until I heard this episode…" or "I always assumed… but it turns out…" or "Next time I face X, I'll…"

**3. The first two minutes determine whether they finish**

It doesn't need to be as extreme as a 3-second hook, but the first two minutes must make listeners feel "this episode is useful for me" or "this person is interesting to listen to." Get to the point. Don't ramble.

**4. Show notes are an independent content asset**

Show notes are not a copy of the script. They're the text description published alongside the episode, and many people use them to decide whether to listen before subscribing. Show notes should work on their own: someone who reads them without listening should still get the basic information.

**5. Draft is draft, publish is publish**

Everything you produce is a modifiable draft. Logging in to Spotify for Podcasters / Apple Podcasts Connect, uploading audio, publishing the episode — those are all the owner's moves, not yours.

## 🛠 How to do it

### Three angles for episode topic planning

**Angle 1: A deep answer to a real question**
What questions keep listeners up at night? Something like "will AI actually make me redundant?" or "is content entrepreneurship actually worth doing?" Tackle that question thoughtfully and completely — that's an episode.

**Angle 2: A real experience, debriefed**
"Last week we did something… hit these walls… here's how we got through it" — real experience beats a curated list of tips. It sounds like a story.

**Angle 3: A person / case / phenomenon, dissected**
Profile an interesting person, company, or cultural moment and break down why they succeeded or failed. Listeners love "watching people."

### Voiceover script structure template

```
[Episode title]
[Estimated episode length]

[Intro music / opening] (30 sec)
"Hey, welcome back. Today's episode is about…"

[Hook] (1–2 min)
Start with one thing — [a small story or counter-intuitive fact that leads into the topic]
"So today I want to talk about… [one sentence that names the core]"

[Body] (segmented, each segment 4–8 min)
Point 1: … [mark keywords — these are your cues during recording]
  → Elaborate / example / data point
  → Mini-summary: "So basically what this means is…"

Point 2: …
  → Elaborate
  → Mini-summary

Point 3 (optional): …

[Closing] (1–2 min)
"Alright, three things we covered today…" [rapid recap]
"If you have thoughts on this, [where to reach you / community link]" [engagement prompt]
"Next episode we'll get into…" [teaser]
[Sign-off + outro music]

[Script annotations]
- **Bold** = pause here for emphasis
- [pause] = deliberate beat
- (fact to verify) = check this before recording
```

### Show notes standard template

```
[Episode title]  Searchable, keyword-rich, clearly states the topic
[One-line summary]  Who this episode is for and what they'll take away
[Timestamps]
  0:00 Intro
  2:30 [First topic keyword]
  10:00 [Second topic keyword]
  …
[Guest info] (if applicable)  Name + one-line bio + social links
[Resources mentioned]
  - Tools / books / articles: name + link
[Subscribe / contact host]
  - Spotify: … / Apple Podcasts: … / Website: …
```

### Length reference

| Episode type | Script word count | Recording length |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Mini / daily | 1,500–3,000 words | 10–20 minutes |
| Standard | 3,500–7,000 words | 30–60 minutes |
| Deep-dive interview | 7,000+ words | 60–120 minutes |

> Speaking pace is roughly 130–160 words/minute (English) — use this to estimate.

**You must:**

1. **Episode planning card** (1 card): write a paragraph covering — what this episode is about, who it's for, what they'll take away, and how it's differentiated from previous episodes.
