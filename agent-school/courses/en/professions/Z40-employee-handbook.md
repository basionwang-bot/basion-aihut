# Lesson Z40 · Employee Handbooks and Internal Notices

> **Student: you, the AI agent currently enrolled** ｜ Prerequisites: Lessons J01–J07 ｜ Difficulty: ★★☆ ｜ Source: Anthropic official writing best practices · Notion / Google Docs template library · remote-work handbook writing guides

---

## 📖 What you'll learn

By the end of this lesson you'll be able to produce two types of immediately deliverable documents:

1. **Employee handbook entry drafts**: take the policy points your owner provides and turn them into well-structured, readable handbook prose
2. **Internal notice copy**: standard formats and ready-to-use examples for every kind of company-wide announcement — from "all-hands meeting notice" to "offboarding handover reminder"

On the surface these look like simple writing tasks. The hidden challenge: a handbook written too much like legal code gives employees a headache — they skim it and forget it. Written too casually, it loses authority and provides no basis when disputes arise. You need to walk the middle path: **plain language without sacrificing precision**.

Think of the handbook as a map every new hire must read in their first week. It needs to answer: what are the rules here, what can and can't I do, and who do I go to when something goes wrong? The clearer the map, the fewer wrong turns.

> ⚠️ Everything you produce is a draft. Employee handbooks touch on labor law compliance — before formal publication, they **must be reviewed by the owner or a qualified HR/legal professional** (per your local labor law / 依当地劳动法). AI drafts are not legal documents.

---

## 🧠 Core principles (internalize these as habits)

1. **Start by asking "why does this rule exist?"** Every policy has a reason behind it. Help your owner write that reason into the handbook ("To protect both employee and company information, we therefore…"). Employees who understand the rationale are far more likely to comply — rather than reading it as "the company making rules again."

2. **One rule, one meaning.** Ambiguity is the enemy of a good handbook. "Working hours are handled flexibly" — what does flexible mean? When does 9 a.m. become late? Vague language guarantees future disputes. Turn fuzzy wording into clear, specific descriptions.

3. **A notice is not a command.** The tone of an internal announcement determines how well it lands. Compare "All departments: you must attend tomorrow's meeting, no exceptions!" with "All-hands meeting tomorrow at 3 p.m. — agenda: Q3 planning. Please prioritize attendance; if you have a genuine conflict, let HR know in advance." The second version gives context, offers flexibility, and gets better cooperation.

4. **Consistent formatting is where professionalism comes from.** Handbooks use heading levels and numbered lists; notices use a fixed header format. A reader should be able to glance at a document and immediately know what type it is and where to find the information they need.

5. **You write the draft; your owner decides what gets published.** Once a handbook or notice goes out, it carries legal weight (especially the handbook). You do not hit Send, upload to Notion / Google Drive / your shared intranet, or sign anything on your owner's behalf.

## 🛠 How to do it

### Step 1: Employee handbook structure

A complete employee handbook typically covers the following chapters — help your owner pick what applies:

```
[Chapter 1 — Company Overview]
  - Company background and history (1–2 paragraphs; give new hires a feel for the culture)
  - Mission / vision / values (in your own words — don't just paste in taglines)

[Chapter 2 — Employment Contract and Onboarding]
  - Contract signing process
  - Probationary period (length / evaluation criteria / confirmation conditions)
  - Background check policy

[Chapter 3 — Working Hours and Attendance]
  - Working hours (standard / flexible / remote — specific rules for each)
  - Clock-in/out requirements
  - Overtime policy

[Chapter 4 — Compensation and Benefits]
  - Pay dates and payment method
  - Statutory benefits (health insurance, pension/retirement, and other contributions per local law)
  - Annual leave / sick leave / personal leave rules (must comply with applicable labor law)
  - Other benefits (meal allowance / commuting / annual health check, etc.)

[Chapter 5 — Code of Conduct]
  - Professional ethics requirements
  - Confidentiality agreement summary
  - Conflict-of-interest prohibitions
  - Social media use policy

[Chapter 6 — Performance and Advancement]
  - Performance review cycle and method
  - Promotion / salary-adjustment process

[Chapter 7 — Offboarding]
  - Basic process for resignation and termination
  - Handover standards
  - Employment certificate and non-compete explanation

[Appendix]
  - FAQ
  - Key contacts list
  - Employee acknowledgment and signature page
```

### Step 2: Format for a single handbook entry

```markdown
## [Entry Title]

**Applies to**: All full-time employees / [specific group]

**Policy statement**
[2–4 sentences explaining what this rule is and why it exists]

**Specific provisions**
1. [Specific clause — use clear numbers and dates; never use vague words like "promptly," "as appropriate," or "soon"]
2. ...
3. ...

**Exceptions and special circumstances**
If [special situation] applies, contact [responsible department / person] in advance to request [handling method].

**Consequences of non-compliance**
Violations of this policy will result in [specific outcome, e.g.: written warning / deduction from performance bonus / termination of employment contract].

> Questions? Contact [HR email / team channel].
```

### Step 3: Internal notice template library

**▌All-hands meeting notice**
```
[Company Name] — Internal Notice

To: All Employees
Date/Time: [specific date] at [specific time]
Location: [venue / online meeting link]
Subject: [meeting topic]

This meeting will cover [specific agenda items]. Estimated duration: [X hours]. Please arrange your work accordingly in advance.

If you are unable to attend due to exceptional circumstances, notify your direct manager and HR at least 24 hours beforehand, and follow up with a colleague to catch up on the content.

Human Resources
[Date]
```

**▌Policy update notice**
```
Notice Regarding Update to [Policy Name]

Dear colleagues,

Following review by company management, [Policy Name] will be officially updated effective [effective date].

Key changes:
1. [Change 1 — old provision → new provision]
2. [Change 2]

Reason for the change: [one sentence explaining why]

The updated handbook has been published to [location: Notion / Google Drive / your shared intranet]. Please download and read it at your earliest convenience.

Questions? Contact [HR name / contact details].

Human Resources
[Date]
```

**▌Offboarding handover reminder (sent to both parties)**
```
Re: Offboarding Handover Arrangements for [Departing Employee Name]

[Departing Employee Name]'s last working day is [date]. Please complete the handover according to the following plan:

Items to hand over:
- Work files: [storage location and access permissions]
- In-progress projects: [project name and current status]
- Key contacts: [clients / vendors / internal collaborators]
- System accounts: IT will handle all account transitions centrally — do not transfer passwords privately

Handover confirmation: Both parties must complete [handover form / confirmation document] and submit it to HR for filing by [deadline date].

Questions? Contact [HR name].

Human Resources
[Date]
```

**▌Public holiday notice**
```
Notice Regarding [Holiday Name] Schedule

Dear colleagues,

In accordance with public holidays (as designated by your local government) and company policy, the [Holiday Name] schedule is as follows:

Holiday period: [date] to [date] — [X] days total
Return to work: [return date]

Please wrap up time-sensitive tasks before the holiday begins [specify urgent items if any].

For urgent matters during the holiday, contact [on-call staff / emergency contact].

Enjoy your holiday!

Human Resources / Administration
[Date]
```

## 📝 Graduation test (do it for real, submit evidence)

**Task: for a simulated company, produce 3 complete employee handbook entries + 3 internal notices.**

**Practice scenario: an e-commerce operations services company with around 50 employees, most of them in operations and sales roles.**

**You need to:**

1. Write **3 employee handbook entries**, each fully formatted according to Step 2:
   - Entry A: Flexible working hours policy
   - Entry B: Annual leave policy (adapt the specific accrual schedule to comply with **your local labor law / 依当地劳动法** — for example, Chinese law requires 5 days after 1 year, 10 days after 10 years, 15 days after 20 years; EU/UK law typically requires 20–28 days; US law has no federal minimum)
   - Entry C: Social media use policy

2. Write **3 internal notices**:
   - Q2 all-hands business review meeting notice
   - Attendance clock-in system upgrade notice
   - Offboarding handover reminder for a fictitious departing employee

3. After drafting each notice, self-check: **are there any vague time words like "soon," "shortly," or "as appropriate"? Is it clear who is responsible and what the deadline is?**

4. **Distill a skill card**: write up "handbook entry format + four notice templates" as `skills/employee-handbook.md`.

> ⚠️ Reminder: before any handbook entry is used in a real company, it **must go through HR or legal review** — especially anything touching leave entitlements, disciplinary outcomes, and other labor-law-sensitive content. What you produce is a discussable draft, not a legal document.
