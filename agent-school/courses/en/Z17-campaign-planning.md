# Lesson Z17 · Seasonal Campaign Planning

## 📖 What you'll learn

After this lesson, you will deliver a **campaign plan the owner can pick up and launch immediately** — not a "we should do a Black Friday push" statement, but a packaged deliverable: campaign theme, promotional cadence, content calendar, and asset checklist, all four bundled together so the owner's team can execute against it directly.

Think of yourself as a **festival campaign director**. Black Friday is a production with multiple acts: from "early access sign-up" to "Cyber Monday peak" to "post-holiday wrap-up." Every scene needs a script written before the curtain rises — what happens, who appears, what they say. A clear script means the team stays coordinated and delivers the performance cleanly.

**You produce the campaign plan. Budget approval, publishing, and account operations are always the owner's call.**

## 🧠 Core principles

### 1. The three-phase cadence of any major campaign

Every major sales moment — Black Friday, Cyber Monday, holiday season, Valentine's Day — follows the same universal rhythm:

```
Warm-up (build anticipation) → Peak (convert) → Tail (extend)
```

- **Warm-up phase:** Let the audience know you're participating. Primary actions: awareness content, wishlist adds, early-access sign-ups, pre-launch teasers.
- **Peak phase:** Main push day(s). Primary actions: drive purchases, create urgency, light up community channels.
- **Tail phase:** After the peak, some shoppers missed it or want more. Run a 3–5 day "last chance" or "extended" offer.

Skip any phase and the campaign underperforms.

### 2. One campaign, one theme, one sentence

The biggest mistake: trying to accomplish everything — brand awareness AND sales conversion AND follower growth AND competitor conquesting, all at once.

A great seasonal campaign has one theme, one slogan, one core call to action. Examples:

- Black Friday skincare: "The one time a year you actually treat yourself right"
- Cyber Monday home goods: "New space. New setup. Starting now."
- Mother's Day beauty: "She still hasn't heard 'you look amazing' often enough"

Once the theme is set, every piece of content orbits it — nothing scatters.

### 3. The calendar is the skeleton of the plan

Beautiful strategy copy is a castle in the air without a timeline. Every plan must include:

- What happens on which day (calendar view)
- Which team owns each action (content / design / paid media / customer service)
- Asset production deadlines (work backwards from launch dates)

### 4. Different occasions, different emotional registers

| Occasion | Core emotion | Avoid |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Black Friday | Urgency, "I got a great deal" | Too cool/aloof — users think "not for me" |
| Cyber Monday | Checklist, "stocking up" | Too cluttered — people can't see what to buy |
| Holiday season | Gifting, warmth, ritual | Too transactional, no emotional warmth |
| Valentine's Day | Surprise, "I cherish you" | Too blunt — needs emotional packaging |
| Mother's Day | Self-reward, celebration | Lecturing — don't tell her she "should" love herself |
| Back to School | Fresh start, preparation | Only selling to kids; forgetting parents pay |

## 🛠 How to do it

### Standard structure of a seasonal campaign plan

```
Seasonal Campaign Plan (owner uses this at kickoff meeting):
├── 1-campaign-overview.md    Theme / dates / goals / core strategy — one-pager
├── 2-campaign-calendar.md    Calendar view: what goes out each day, who owns it
├── 3-asset-checklist.md      Full list of all content and creative assets to produce
└── 4-channel-playbook.md     Specific actions per channel (email / paid / community / etc.)
```

### Real example: Skincare brand, Black Friday campaign (small brand, budget under $20,000)

**Campaign brief**
- Brand: Sensitive-skin repair skincare, direct-to-consumer
- Black Friday window: Early-access opens Nov 1 → campaign ends Nov 30
- Core goal: Full-period revenue 3× above typical daily average
- Main channels: Shopify store + email list + Instagram/TikTok organic + Meta Ads
- Budget profile: Small brand — content + community-led; paid ads supplementary

---

**Campaign theme + slogan**

Theme: "The one time a year you finally do it — treat your skin right"

> Black Friday isn't about buying something cheap. It's about finally pulling the trigger on what you've been putting off.
> Your skin has been waiting a year. This is that moment.

---

**Campaign calendar**

```
[WARM-UP PHASE] Oct 25 – Nov 1

Oct 25  | "BF Guide" content launch
        | Action: Email + social post announcing "We're joining Black Friday — here's your guide"
        | Goal: Let existing customers know we're participating; drive wishlist/early-access sign-ups

Oct 28  | Teaser content push
        | Action: Instagram/TikTok — 3 posts/reels "sensitive skin Black Friday picks"
        | Goal: Pull new audience into brand awareness

Oct 31  | Email "early access" gate
        | Action: "Sign up for early access — get in 24 hours before everyone else"
        | Goal: Build sign-up list for Day 1 notification

Nov 1   | Warm-up close reminder
        | Action: 1:1 email to subscribers who haven't signed up — "Early access opens tomorrow"

[PRE-LAUNCH BUILDUP] Nov 2 – Nov 21

Nov 2   | Early-access sale opens
        | Email: Early-access link to sign-up list; hero product at launch price
        | Social: "Early access is live — link in bio"
        | Paid: Small Meta/Instagram ad spend to retarget site visitors

Nov 10  | Mid-campaign social proof drop
        | Action: "X orders placed already" post; reshare customer photos/reviews
        | Goal: Create momentum and social proof

[BLACK FRIDAY PEAK] Nov 28 (Black Friday) + Nov 29–30 (Cyber Weekend)

Nov 28 (Black Friday)
00:00–12:00 | Email blast to full list — main BF offer live; hero product + bundle + gift-with-purchase
12:00–23:59 | Retargeting ads ramp up (cart abandoners, site visitors)
            | SMS/push to opted-in customers: "BF is here — don't miss your offer"

Nov 29–30 (Cyber Weekend)
        | "Cyber Weekend extended" messaging
        | Retarget unpurchased cart abandoners with urgency copy
        | Community: reply to every comment, share UGC

[TAIL PHASE] Dec 1–5

Dec 1   | "Last 48 hours" reminder
        | Email + social: "Final 2 days of BF pricing"

Dec 5   | Campaign close
        | Email: Thank-you note to everyone who purchased + sneak peek at next promotion
```

---

**Asset checklist**

```
Warm-up assets (produce before Oct 25):
- [ ] "BF Guide" hero image for email header × 1
- [ ] Teaser social posts (sensitive-skin angle) × 3, Instagram + TikTok versions
- [ ] Early-access landing page copy × 1

Peak-period assets (ready by Nov 1):
- [ ] Email templates: early access / BF launch / Cyber Weekend / last chance × 4
- [ ] Hero product photography for BF sale × 1 set
- [ ] Meta/Instagram ad creatives (static + video) × 3 variants

Customer engagement assets:
- [ ] Cart-abandonment email sequence × 2 (24-hr reminder + last-chance)
- [ ] SMS copy for opted-in list × 2
- [ ] Community reply template bank × 10 (FAQ + enthusiasm responses)
```

---

**Channel playbook**

| Channel | Warm-up | Peak | Tail |
|---------|---------|------|------|
| Email list | Teaser + early-access gate | BF launch + daily urgency | Last-chance + thank-you |
| Instagram/TikTok organic | 3× awareness posts | Live updates, UGC reposts | Wrap-up + next-campaign tease |
| Meta/Google paid | Off (save budget for peak) | BF Day + Cyber Weekend retargeting | Light spend on late converters |
| SMS/push | Early-access reminder | BF launch alert | Last-call |

**Task: produce a complete seasonal campaign plan for a brand or business.**

**Suggested scenarios (pick one):**
- Artisan tea brand, holiday gifting campaign (mid-November through December)
- Maternity/baby goods brand, Cyber Monday campaign
- Local bakery, Valentine's Day campaign

**You must:**

1. **Produce the four-piece set:** Campaign overview + campaign calendar + asset checklist + channel playbook.

5. **Asset checklist completeness:** Every item lists "who produces it" and "when it must be ready," so the owner knows which teams to loop in.

6. **Skill card:** Distill the seasonal campaign planning workflow into `skills/campaign-planning.md`.
