## 📖 What You'll Learn

By the end of this lesson, you'll produce a **ready-to-use live commerce content pack** your host can pick up and run with — not a vague suggestion like "you could talk about the product like this," but a minute-by-minute stream script, a scene-based sales talk library, and a pacing cue sheet, all bundled together.

Think of yourself as the **floor director** standing just off camera, holding a densely annotated cue sheet: what to say in this segment, which line to hit when a certain moment arrives, what to shout when inventory is running low. The creator is watching the lens and talking; you've pre-arranged every minute for them. Their voice, your brain — that's the golden team.

**Your job ends when the script files are finished and placed in the host's hands.** Going live, collabing with a creator, taking real orders — those always belong to the host or their production team, never to you.

## 🧠 Core Principles

### 1. Live Shopping Is a "Time-for-Trust" Exchange

A live stream is nothing like a written post. Readers can scroll back whenever they want; viewers cannot — if they zone out for ten seconds, that piece of information is gone forever. That's why **critical information must be repeated, and key moments must be emphasized three times**. Your script needs to mark every emphasis point with a clear stage direction: "say this with weight — pause here."

### 2. Pacing Matters More Than Content

For a 90-minute stream, the structure typically looks like this:

- **0–10 min** — Warm-up: build the room, greet newcomers, preview today's highlights
- **10–60 min** — Product showcase: cycle through featured items
- **60–80 min** — Urgency close: the high-energy push to buy
- **80–90 min** — Fan appreciation + sign-off teaser

Scripts must be **labeled by the minute** so the creator glances at the cue sheet and instantly knows "where am I right now, and what's the next move."

### 3. Compliance Lines You Must Never Cross

Saying "the absolute cheapest," "lowest price on the internet," or "guaranteed authentic" violates platform rules on TikTok Shop, Instagram Shopping, and Amazon Live alike. Fabricating "limited time only" or "only 3 left" to manufacture fake urgency is equally prohibited. **Compliant urgency comes from real value and genuine emotional connection — not deception.**

Platform prohibited-word lists update regularly. Whenever a script draft runs into a superlative or unverifiable claim, swap it for a factual, FTC-compliant alternative. Both the FTC's Endorsement Guides and each platform's own commerce policies (TikTok Community Guidelines, Instagram Commerce Policies, Amazon Live Creator guidelines) apply.

### 4. Sales Talk Must Be Localized to the Creator's Style

Is the creator high-energy and direct, or warm and conversational? Are they selling skincare or kitchen gadgets? Is the audience homemakers, Gen Z, or fitness enthusiasts? You need clear answers to those three questions before you write a single line — generic templates that don't fit the creator's voice are unusable.

## 🛠 How to Do It

### Standard Script Format

```
[STREAM HEADER]
- Creator name / stream theme / estimated duration
- Featured product list (1–3 hero products + supporting items)
- Today's hook offer (the reason viewers stay)

[MINUTE-BY-MINUTE SCRIPT]

[00:00–05:00] Warm-Up
  - Creator says: "Hey everyone, welcome to [Channel Name] — so glad you're here…"
  - Stage direction: move the camera to show the setup; have the co-host display products; pin [TODAY'S DEAL] in comments
  - Hook line: "If you stay for 10 minutes today, I've got a surprise just for you…"

[05:00–20:00] Product Showcase: [Product #1 — Hero Item]
  - Pain-point open: "Have you ever dealt with…?"
  - Three product highlights (each with a concrete example)
  - Price anchor: "Retail on our website is normally $XX — today's live-stream exclusive is…"
  - Call to action: "Tap the link below and add to cart — don't go anywhere!"
  (…repeat pattern for each product)

[XX:XX–XX:XX] Urgency Close
  - Core lines: see Sales Talk Library below
  - Stage direction: 3-minute countdown, co-host echoes the CTA, respond to live comments

[SIGN-OFF TEASER]
  - Next stream date / preview of next featured product
```

### Sales Talk Library (Reusable Templates)

**① Warm-Up Open**
> "Hey, long-time viewers — thanks for always showing up. If you're new here, hit that follow button; you don't have to buy anything today — just come hang and learn something. I do want to give you a heads-up: I have a few items I've been holding back for a while. I checked the warehouse this morning and stock is genuinely limited on one of them…"

**② Product Showcase — Pain-Point Hook**
> "I know exactly what you're worried about when buying [product category] online — you either don't see results, or it arrives and you realize you made the wrong call, right? Let me just put this in front of you and walk you through what I actually found…"

**③ Price Anchor (Compliant Version)**
> "This item lists at $XX on our website, and that's what it goes for year-round. For today's live stream, we were able to set up a stream-exclusive price — $XX, just for this session."

**④ Urgency Close (Compliant Version)**
> "I'll say this once more: we brought [X] units to this stream. I'm not trying to pressure you — last time we went live, the last twenty minutes wiped out our remaining stock and a lot of people missed out. If you're already thinking about it, don't wait. Hit the link, add it to your cart now, and we'll do the final checkout together in a few minutes."

**⑤ Engagement Hook (Keep Viewers in the Room)**
> "Drop a comment and tell me what city you're watching from — it seriously helps the stream reach more people, and I love seeing where everyone's tuning in from."

**⑥ Sign-Off**
> "To everyone who spent [X] hours here with me today — thank you so much, this means everything. Next stream is [date], and I have something bigger planned — follow now and you'll get a notification when we go live. See you then!"
