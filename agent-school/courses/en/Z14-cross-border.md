# Lesson Z14 · Cross-Border E-Commerce Listings & Localization

## 📖 What You'll Learn

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to produce a **complete, platform-ready listing content pack** for a cross-border product — not a machine-translated rough draft, but title, bullet points, and description copy that actually meet Amazon and TikTok Shop standards, plus a "bilingual reference sheet" your principal can read and verify.

Picture yourself as a copywriter at an international trading company. The product is solid — good manufacturing, reasonable price — but whoever wrote the current description clearly just ran it through a translator. Overseas shoppers open the page and immediately click away. Your job is to dress this product up properly for its new market: not hype, not word-for-word translation, but copy that speaks in the local buyer's language and answers the one question they're always asking: *"What does this do for me, and why should I pick this one?"*

**Important thresholds for cross-border sellers:**
- Amazon seller accounts require phone number and address verification for each marketplace; some categories also require a local payment method — **these are your principal's prerequisites, not your responsibility**
- TikTok Shop Global Shop is open to international sellers with relatively low entry barriers — a good starting point for new cross-border sellers
- This lesson focuses entirely on **content production**; store setup, live listings, and ad campaigns are your principal's decisions to execute

## 🧠 Core Principles

### 1. Localization ≠ Translation — It's a Mindset Switch

The feature-dump mindset describes a product like this: "Made from Japanese-imported fabric, processed through three finishing stages, certified to ISO 9001…"

The buyer's market mindset is what actually sells: *"Silky-smooth against your skin, stays fresh all day even in summer."*

The switch: **replace "what it is" with "what it means for you."**

This is the difference between shouting specs at someone and having a conversation with them. Buyers don't read listings to learn about your manufacturing process — they read to find out whether this product solves their problem.

### 2. Amazon's Algorithm Reads Keywords First; Humans Read Copy Second

Amazon's A9 algorithm ranks listings by keyword relevance. Great listings start with keyword research (use tools like Helium 10 or Jungle Scout), then weave those terms naturally into the copy. Keyword stuffing gets you penalized by the platform *and* drives buyers away — both bad outcomes.

TikTok Shop skews heavily toward short video and live commerce. The listing copy is shorter, but hashtags and video scripts carry more weight than text descriptions.

### 3. Bullet Points Are the First Visual Battlefield

Most Amazon shoppers never read the product description — they look at images and bullet points, then decide. So:
- Open each bullet with a **capitalized feature phrase** (e.g., `ULTRA-THIN DESIGN –`)
- Each bullet delivers **one complete benefit**, kept to 2 lines or fewer
- At least 2 of your 5 bullets should directly address common buyer pain points

### 4. The Bilingual Reference Sheet Is for Your Principal, Not the Platform

In your content pack, the English copy goes to the platform; the explanatory notes go to your principal. They need to read through the side-by-side comparison and understand what each line of English is actually saying — and whether anything has gone wrong or needs adjustment. This annotated handoff is where your professional value shows.

## 🛠 How to Do It

### Standard Content Pack Structure (Amazon Version)

```
[Product Brief] (filled in by your principal — you produce from this)
- Product name
- Core function (one sentence)
- Target buyer
- Competitor listing URL(s) (for keyword and style benchmarking)
- Marketplace: Amazon US / UK / JP / etc.

[Deliverables]

① Title
   Rule: under 200 characters, contains primary keyword, title case
   Annotation: [logic and keyword placement for this title]
   English copy: [ready-to-use English title]

② Bullet Points × 5
   Annotation + English copy, side by side for each bullet

③ Product Description (Description / A+ Content)
   Annotation + English copy
   Length: 150–400 words, 2–3 paragraphs

④ Backend Search Keywords
   Under 250 bytes, comma-separated, for backend Seller Central entry
   Annotation: purpose note for each keyword cluster

⑤ TikTok Shop Version (if needed)
   - Short title (under 80 characters)
   - Product selling points (3 bullets, conversational tone)
   - Video script (15–30 seconds)
```

### Worked Example: Diatomite Bath Mat (Amazon US)

**Product brief:**
- Product: Diatomite quick-dry bath mat, 40×60 cm, gray
- Target buyer: US households, adults 25–45
- Core advantages: ultra-fast drying, non-slip, easy to clean

---

**① Title (English copy)**
```
Diatomite Bath Mat 16"x24" - Fast Drying Stone Bath Rug, Non-Slip Shower Mat for Bathroom Floor, Easy to Clean, Gray
```
**Annotation:** Primary keyword "Diatomite Bath Mat" leads; dimensions converted to inches (US buyers don't think in centimeters); three core benefits — Fast Drying / Non-Slip / Easy to Clean — ordered by buyer decision weight.

---

**② Bullet Points (English copy + annotation)**

| # | English Copy | Annotation |
|---|-------------|------------|
| 1 | **DRIES IN SECONDS, NOT HOURS** – Made from natural diatomaceous earth, this bath mat absorbs moisture 3x faster than fabric mats. Step out of the shower and feel dry ground instantly. | Fast drying is the #1 pain point; the "3x faster" comparison adds credibility through data |
| 2 | **STAYS PUT, EVEN ON WET FLOORS** – The non-slip rubber bottom grips any bathroom tile or hardwood floor. No sliding, no accidents—safe for kids and seniors. | Non-slip is framed as a safety story — widens appeal to parents and those caring for older family members |
| 3 | **ONE RINSE, GOOD AS NEW** – Unlike fabric mats that trap mold and odors, our stone mat is self-cleaning. Just rinse under water and let the sun do the rest. | Easy cleaning is contrasted against a concrete fabric-mat pain point (mold, smell) with a scene the buyer can picture |
| 4 | **A MATCH FOR ANY BATHROOM STYLE** – The minimalist gray design fits modern, boho, or farmhouse bathrooms. Looks as good as it works. | Addresses buyers who care about home aesthetics — a significant segment in the US home goods market |
| 5 | **BUILT TO LAST YEARS** – Chipping? Cracking? Not here. Our solid diatomite core is reinforced to handle daily use for 3+ years without degrading. | Durability is a high-weight concern for US buyers; naming the specific fear ("chipping, cracking") then dismissing it is more persuasive than a generic durability claim |

---

**③ Product Description (English copy)**
```
Tired of soggy bath mats that take forever to dry — and smell like they've been sitting in a gym locker? 

The DiaCare Stone Bath Mat changes everything. Carved from 100% natural diatomaceous earth, it soaks up water in seconds and air-dries completely between uses. No musty smell, no washing machine trips, no waiting.

The solid stone construction means this mat won't slip, bunch up, or wear out. Wipe it down, rinse it off, set it in the sun — that's the entire care routine. It's the last bath mat you'll need to buy.

Dimensions: 16" x 24" (40 x 60 cm) | Color: Stone Gray | Safe for all bathroom floor types
```
**Annotation:** Opens with a pain-point question to hook the reader and avoid a dull intro. "Gym locker" is a culturally specific image that lands immediately for a US audience. The closing spec line doubles as searchable text that algorithms can index.

---

**④ TikTok Shop Video Script (15-second version)**

```
English copy:
"Your bath mat is probably growing mold right now. [hold up wet fabric mat] Gross, right? 
Switch to a stone bath mat — absorbs water in 3 seconds, dries itself, never smells. 
We ship directly from our factory. Link below."

Annotation:
The opening "shock hook" is the standard TikTok short-video opener — disrupt, then solve.
"Ships directly from our factory" is a trust signal that works well for cross-border sellers 
on TikTok Shop; it implies price advantage and production control.
The whole script hits three beats in under 15 seconds: pain point → solution → call to action.
```

**Task: produce a complete cross-border listing content pack for one product.**

**Choose one scenario:**
- Stainless steel insulated tumbler → Amazon US

**What you need to deliver:**

1. **Complete content pack:** title + 5 bullet points + product description + backend search keywords + bilingual annotation sheet.
